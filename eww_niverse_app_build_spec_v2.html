Game design — core loops
The two-loop architecture
Every retention-positive game splits into a short cycle (immediate dopamine) and a long cycle (long-term motivation). EWW-niverse uses Discovery as the short loop and Mastery as the long loop. They feed each other, which is how a kid stays engaged for weeks instead of one session.
Discovery loop (short)
Open app → use scan → reveal silhouette → creature classified → see basic fact + EWW-meter. ~30 seconds of dopamine per cycle. Triggered multiple times per session.
Mastery loop (long)
Play Lab quiz → earn scans + classify correctly on creatures already classified → bonus fact unlocks → progress to next EWW-stage → unlock new mechanics. Days to weeks of progression.
Scans — the in-game currency
All creature unlocks cost scans. Scans are the throttle. Free tier earns ~7 scans per active day, Full Lab Pass earns ~14. This pacing means a kid can't binge through the entire library in one sitting and lose interest. Replenishment creates the return habit.

The five gamification mechanics
Daily Specimen
One creature highlighted on the home screen each day. Classifying it within 24 hours = 2x scan reward. Drives daily opens without nagging notifications. Rotates server-side via Cloud Function on a 24-hour cron. Free tier: rotates Creepy Creatures only. Paid: rotates all unlocked books.
Specimen Sets (sub-collections)
Themed sub-groups inside each book that the kid completes by classifying every member. Examples: "Parasites" (8 creatures), "Things With Too Many Legs" (12), "Skin Crawlers" (10), "Slime Producers" (6). Each completed set rewards one Contaminated Specimen + a Set badge. Free tier sees only Creepy Creatures sets. Adds discoverable medium-term goals.
Lab Missions (quests)
Small in-game objectives, displayed on the HUD. "Classify 3 Epic creatures this week." "Achieve a 7-question streak." "Master 10 creatures." Each rewards scans, progression points, or Special Specimens. Weekly rotation in paid tier. Free tier gets a starter set of ~5 fixed missions to introduce the mechanic.
Streak meter
Daily login streak. Shown on the HUD. 7-day milestone rewards a free scan (free tier) or guaranteed Contaminated Specimen (paid tier). Streak resets if a day is skipped — painful enough to bring kids back. No grace period in MVP, can add a single-day pardon later if it feels punishing in playtest.
Personal Records (HUD card)
Visible bragging surface. Fastest quiz time, longest streak, total Contamination Events triggered, total Mastered count, current EWW-stage. Lives on the Recruit File tab. Updates in real time. This is what kids screenshot and show their friends.
Stage progression — what actually unlocks
Each EWW-stage changes the game, not just the badge. Free tier caps at Stage 2. Paid unlocks all five.

Quiz mechanics — three types defined
Gross Fact → Name free + paid
The starter quiz. Show the gross fact at the top. Three creature silhouettes below as options. Kid taps which creature the fact describes. Silhouettes show partial features as visual clues. Works for both classified and unclassified creatures — meaning quizzes drive discovery too.
EWW-meter Ranking paid
Three creature cards appear in a row. Kid drags them into order from least gross to most gross (60% → 80% → 100%). The arcs animate to reveal correctness. Drag interactions make this feel different from tap-to-answer.
Specimen Match paid
A gross fact appears at the top. Three silhouettes below with subtle distinct features (leg shape, antenna count, body outline). Kid matches the fact to the silhouette. Timed, faster = more points. Rapid-fire format runs 8 questions per session for high tempo.
Contamination Event — the signature mechanic
Trigger: 3 correct quiz answers in a row without using a hint.
Visual: Screen glitches green for 2 seconds. All silhouettes briefly flash visible. Audio cascade (sfx_contamination) plays.
Reward: ONE random Contaminated Specimen unlocks automatically. Cannot be obtained any other way. Adds to the kid's collection with a unique green-glow border in the grid.
Why it matters: Skill-based reward, scarcity, conversation surface ("I got a Contaminated Specimen today"). Free tier: only Common-rarity Contaminated unlocks. Paid: all rarities including Epic.
Special Specimens — app-exclusive collection layer
The 7th "book" that doesn't exist on paper
A dedicated pool of ~30 creatures and weird phenomena that exist only in the app. These are not in any printed book. They're earned through gameplay mechanics — never purchased. This is what makes the app a real product beyond a digital version of the books. A kid who owns all 6 physical books still needs the app to collect Special Specimens.

Sound design — pragmatic 3-tier approach
Tier 1 — Sourced (free, commercial license)~15 sounds
Use for: ambient lab loops, navigation clicks, paper slides, mechanical clicks, water/liquid effects, general UI feedback. Sources: Mixkit (free for commercial use, no attribution), Pixabay Sound Effects (CC0 license), Zapsplat ($5/mo paid tier removes attribution). Total time investment: ~3 hours of browsing and downloading.
Tier 2 — AI-generated~4-5 sounds
Use for: the three EWW-meter fill sounds (60%/80%/100%), quiz feedback sounds, declassify sound. Tool: ElevenLabs Sound Effects — text-to-SFX. Free tier gives enough credits for 10-15 short clips. Better output than producer.ai for short stylized SFX, accepts natural language prompts. Total time investment: ~2 hours including iteration.
Tier 3 — Custom production3 signature sounds
Custom production reserved for: sfx_contamination (the Event sound, most important in the app), sfx_pass_unlock (the purchase moment), sfx_stage_up (progression). These three define the app sonically. Source the rest. Total time investment: one evening per sound, ~6 hours total.
Total sound production time: ~11 hours instead of producing all 22 custom (~25-30 hours). The three signature sounds carry the brand character; the sourced and AI-generated sounds carry the functional load.
Auth + COPPA (unchanged)
Anonymous-first flow
App creates anonymous Firebase Auth user on first launch. No PII. Kid plays the entire free tier without identification. Parent email gate appears only at paywall or cloud-save request. Email link sign-in (passwordless) satisfies COPPA's "verifiable parental consent" requirement. Anonymous-to-permanent account linking migrates all progress. Child profile uses codename only, no DOB, no real name. See v1 build spec canvas for full flow detail.

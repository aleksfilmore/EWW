Website plan new
Purpose
Four jobs. One: brand presence and credibility for parents researching the books or app. Two: book catalogue with affiliate links to Amazon and other retailers (revenue stream independent of the app). Three: app landing page with download links and feature explanations. Four: content engine via the Specimen Files blog area, building SEO traffic in the "weird facts for kids" space and giving the brand a publishing rhythm beyond book launches.
Aesthetic — warm parchment, book-aligned
The website lives in the visual language of the books. Cream parchment backgrounds, slime green accents, Cantora One headings, Lexend Deca body. Dr. Icky character illustrations used generously throughout. This is the public face of the brand — the same face the books wear on bookstore shelves. The dark lab aesthetic is reserved exclusively for the app.

Specimen Files content strategy
Weekly publishing rhythm. Three content formats. (1) Specimen of the Week — a deep dive on one creature in Dr. Icky's voice, more detail than the book entry, written for kids who want to know more. (2) Field Reports — short essays from Dr. Icky about weird places, weird discoveries, recent real-world science news adapted for the audience. (3) Reader Submissions — moderated, parent-verified questions from kids answered in character. The blog is also the natural surface for occasional brand moments like new book announcements and app updates.

V1 launch book changed
Switched: Creepy Creatures (was Creepy Tiny World)
Three reasons. One: Creepy Creatures is the franchise flagship — the iconic spider on the EWW-niverse cover comes from this book. Two: broader mass appeal at launch — spiders, snakes, and visible insects have wider recognition than microscopic creatures, which matters when you need maximum word-of-mouth in the first months. Three: practical readiness — the 75 entries and matching illustrations are already organized for this book. Tiny World moves to v2 or v3 as a content drop.

Design language
Two surfaces, one universe
Website + books: warm parchment, character-led, abundant Dr. Icky presence. App: dark lab, selective character use, atmospheric. Same illustration library, different background treatments.
Typography (both surfaces)
Cantora One for display and headings — already used in the books. Lexend Deca for body. Both Google Fonts, zero licensing cost.
App palette (dark lab)
Background #0E1210 · Surface #1A1F1C · Card #222924 · Slime green #5DB84A · Warning amber #D48B1A · Text primary #E6E0CC · Text muted #8A9E86. No white backgrounds anywhere.
Website palette (warm parchment)
Cream parchment background, slime green accent (#5DB84A consistent with app), warm brown body text, dark green headers. Pull from the books' published colour treatment.
App stage evolution
Five interface stages tied to EWW-meter user progression: Kinda Curious (clean dark), Properly Revolted (green haze at edges), Super Slimy (specimen jar background elements), Biologically Alarmed (ambient slow animations), Full Dr. Icky (maximum lab atmosphere).

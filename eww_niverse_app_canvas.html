EWW-niverse app planning canvas

Design language
Concept: the inside of the lab
The books are the entrance — warm parchment, slime drips, colorful creatures. The app is what's behind the door. Darker, more atmospheric, lab equipment aesthetic. Same brand DNA but different register. Opening the app should feel like being granted access to something not meant for everyone.
Typography
Display / headings: Cantora One (Google Fonts) — already used in the books. Continuity with the physical product is a brand asset. Body / all other text: Lexend Deca (Google Fonts) — clean, highly legible, designed for reading comfort. Kids are reading actual scientific content; legibility is non-negotiable. Both fonts load from Google Fonts at zero cost.

Near-black with faint green tint — biological, not sterile. Slime green is the single accent. Amber for EWW-meter 60% tier and warnings only. Text primary is warm off-white, echoing parchment. No white backgrounds anywhere in the app.
UI metaphor — specimen collection
Cards are specimen trays. The creature grid is a collection cabinet. Locked creatures: dark silhouettes labeled UNCLASSIFIED. EWW-meter arc looks like a measuring gauge. Navigation tabs feel like instrument panel switches. Buttons are specimen labels — rectangular, slightly rough-edged, not rounded pills. Empty states: "No specimens classified yet." Loading: "Cross-referencing specimen database..."

Sound design inventory
Sonic palette — apply to every sound
Low frequencies dominant (80–300Hz for impact sounds). Wet and biological textures: drips, viscous pours, squelches, organic pressure. Lab equipment reference: relays, heavy switches, pressure gauges. Avoid entirely: major chords, rising pitch rewards, cheerful dings, cartoon wet sounds (too comedic), pure digital beeps. Format for all one-shot files: mono or stereo .mp3 at 44.1kHz, normalised to -6dBFS peak. Loop files: seamless with 2-second crossfade tail. All files named per the ID column below.
Ambient loops
IDNameDurationCharacter + production notes
amb_home Home / EWW-niverse loop 45–60s loop Very low background hum (40–60Hz), barely audible. One slow distant drip every 8–12 seconds at randomised intervals. Faint wet bubbling in far background. Volume should sit below conscious awareness — felt more than heard. Seamless loop. This plays while the kid browses the creature grid.
amb_lab The Lab / quiz loop 30–45s loop Slightly higher frequency than amb_home — more alert, more focused. A faint slow ticking or metronomic pulse underneath (think lab timer, not clock). Suggests precision. The shift from amb_home to amb_lab should be noticeable but not jarring — crossfade over 1 second when tab switches.
amb_launch App launch swell 3–4s, plays once Silence, then a low hum rises from nothing to a steady state over 2 seconds, like electrical equipment powering up. Not a musical note — a functional sound. Fades smoothly into amb_home after the splash screen clears.
Navigation
IDNameDurationCharacter + production notes
sfx_tab Tab switch 0.08s A single heavy relay click or rotary switch engagement. Mechanical, dry, no reverb. Think industrial instrument panel, not phone UI. Very short. Should register as physical without being distracting.
sfx_scroll Grid scroll Continuous Optional. A faint slow ticking as cards pass — like specimen trays sliding on a rack. Very low volume. Can be omitted if it feels distracting in testing. Decide after first prototype playtest.
Creature interactions
IDNameDurationCharacter + production notes
sfx_card_tap Creature card tap 0.15s Soft wet tap — like something shifting inside a sealed container. Low-mid frequency thud with a brief damp tail. Not alarming, not satisfying. Just present. The difference between tapping a living thing and tapping glass.
sfx_locked Locked creature tap 0.3s A dull sealed thud — like pressing on a container that won't open. Slightly lower and duller than sfx_card_tap. Add a very brief low buzz or resonance tail (0.1s) to suggest something is inside but contained. Final-feeling.
sfx_unlock Creature unlock sequence 2.5–3s Three-phase sequence synced to the Lottie jar animation. Phase 1 (0–0.5s): dry glass cracking — a sharp crackling with no reverb, like a specimen jar under pressure. Phase 2 (0.5–1.5s): wet liquid release — a rushing viscous pour or pressurised hiss, biological not clean. Phase 3 (1.5–2.5s): a final wet settling thud as the creature card slots into the grid. This is the primary reward sound — it needs to feel genuinely satisfying.
sfx_detail_open Creature detail screen open 0.4s A slow, heavy drawer or tray sliding open — lab specimen drawer being pulled out. Low frequency slide with a soft stop at the end. This plays as the detail screen animates in.
sfx_declassify Bonus fact reveal 0.8–1s The sound of a classified document being unsealed — a dry paper slide (the redacted bars moving) followed by a brief low electronic confirmation tone as the text appears. The paper slide carries most of the character. The tone is very short, one note, not musical.
EWW-meter
IDNameDurationCharacter + production notes
sfx_meter_60 Kinda Revolting fill 1.2s A slow viscous pour — like thick liquid rising in a tube — that stops abruptly at 60% with a small squelch. Should feel reluctant, like it doesn't quite want to move. The squelch at the end is the tier's character.
sfx_meter_80 Super Slimy fill 1.5s Similar pour but more fluid and inevitable. Stops at 80% with a wet splat rather than a squelch — slightly more impact. A hint of pressure in the sound, like the container is fuller.
sfx_meter_100 Total Barf fill 2s Full fill to 100% with a brief overpressure moment — the liquid rushes in, hits the top, there is a half-second of overflow or gurgle before settling. The overshoot-and-settle mirrors the Lottie arc animation. This should be the most dramatic of the three meter sounds.
sfx_meter_pulse Meter pulse (correct answer) 0.3s A very brief version of the fill sound — just the start of sfx_meter_60 cut at 0.3s. Used on the meter arc pulse during a correct quiz answer. Should feel like a gauge reacting to a reading.
Quiz / The Lab
IDNameDurationCharacter + production notes
sfx_question Quiz question appears 0.25s A brief data-transmission sound — like a classified file being decrypted. Short burst of digital texture followed by a clean click as the question card settles. Not musical, functional.
sfx_answer_tap Answer option tap 0.1s A dry mechanical click — like placing a specimen tray into a rack slot. Heavier and more deliberate than a standard UI tap. No reverb. The kid should feel they are selecting something physical.
sfx_correct Correct answer 0.4s A low satisfying thud — 150–200Hz dominant. Think a heavy rubber stamp landing on paper, or a specimen container being sealed correctly. The pitch is low enough that it registers as authority rather than celebration. No high-frequency components, no musical character. Followed by sfx_meter_pulse.
sfx_wrong Wrong answer 0.2s or silence Two options — decide in testing. Option A: a very brief low-frequency denial buzz (80–100Hz, 0.2s) like a lock refusing to turn. Option B: pure silence — the visual red pulse does all the work. Silence is more unsettling and more consistent with the tone. Recommended starting point: silence. Add sfx_wrong only if playtesting shows kids need the audio feedback.
sfx_contamination Contamination Event (3-streak) 3.5–4s total The most complex and important sound in the app — five phases. Phase 1 (0–0.3s): abrupt radio/signal interference burst, like a transmission cutting out. Phase 2 (0.3–0.8s): a low subsonic rumble building, the feeling of something wrong spreading. Phase 3 (0.8–2s): a wet biological cascade — dripping turning to pouring, organic and uncontrolled. Phase 4 (2–2.5s): sudden silence. Phase 5 (2.5–3.5s): a single low electronic tone as the bonus fact appears — one note, measured, like a result being confirmed. Each phase should be distinct. The silence in Phase 4 is as important as the sounds around it.
sfx_quiz_complete Lab session complete 1.5s The sound of a lab instrument completing its cycle — a measured sequence of two low tones (not a fanfare, not ascending — equal pitch or slightly descending) followed by a mechanical click of completion. Clinical and final. "Experiment concluded" not "You won."
Progression events
IDNameDurationCharacter + production notes
sfx_stage_up Stage transition (level up) 3s A full-screen event deserves a substantial sound. Three phases. Phase 1 (0–1s): a low building pressure hum, like a system under increasing load. Phase 2 (1–1.5s): a single heavy mechanical lock or vault-click — the threshold moment. Phase 3 (1.5–3s): a brief ambient wash as the new darker stage aesthetic settles in, fading into the updated ambient loop. Should feel like a door opening deeper into the lab.
Onboarding and access
IDNameDurationCharacter + production notes
sfx_transmission Onboarding text appearance Continuous while text types A slow data-feed typewriter texture — not the Hollywood typewriter clatter. More like intermittent data bursts, each burst 0.05–0.1s, with irregular gaps between them. Plays under the green monospace text appearing on the black screen. Should feel like receiving a classified transmission, not typing.
sfx_access_granted Access Granted 0.8s A clean biometric-style lock release — a brief scan tone (0.2s, low pitch) followed by a satisfying mechanical unlatch (0.3s) and a short resonance tail (0.3s). Should feel earned and slightly ceremonial without being celebratory. Used at onboarding completion and at Full Lab Pass purchase.
Unlock and payment
IDNameDurationCharacter + production notes
sfx_pass_unlock Full Lab Pass purchased 2s More substantial than sfx_access_granted — this is the biggest unlock in the app. A sealed specimen collection being opened for the first time: a heavy seal breaking (0.3s), a rush of pressurised air or liquid (0.5s), then a cascade of short unlock clicks as multiple books become available (0.7s), settling into a held low resonance (0.5s). The sequence should feel like a vault opening, not a purchase completing.
sfx_code_accept Bundle code accepted 1s A brief scan-and-verify sequence — a short processing texture (0.3s) then a version of sfx_access_granted scaled down (0.7s). Confirms one book unlocking. Less dramatic than sfx_pass_unlock.
sfx_code_reject Bundle code rejected 0.3s A clean definitive denial — like a key failing to turn in a lock. A short low-frequency clunk with no resonance tail. Brief, final, not harsh. No buzz, no alarm quality. The lock simply did not open.

App sections (v1)
EWW-niverse core
450 entries by book. Cards: illustration, gross fact, animated arc, habitat, locked bonus fact. Grid fills progressively. Locked entries show as dark silhouettes — UNCLASSIFIED.
The Lab — quiz engine core
Gross Fact → Name (free). EWW-meter ranking + Specimen Match (Full Lab Pass). Contamination Event on 3-streak. Wrong answers: "Incorrect. Recalibrate." No encouragement copy.

Parent gate at account creation. Kid explores free tier first. Parent email required at paywall or progress save. No DOB collected, no PII on child record.
